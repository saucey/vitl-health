Even for the 5.5% of people that consistently manage to get their 5-a-day, is that really all it takes to get the recommended amount of all 13 vitamins and 16 minerals the body needs?

Not only do we eat less than our hunter-gatherer ancestors (scientists estimate they were eating 3,000-4,000Kcal a day to fuel their active lifestyles), resulting in a lower intake of vitamins and minerals, some speculate that our food is actually less nutritious than it used to be. According to the Earth Summit Report (1992), the quantity of nutrients in our soil could be up to 85% less than it was 100 years ago as a result of intense modern farming methods.

Whether or not our diet is able to provide all that we need for optimal health, there will always be factors beyond our immediate control that affect our individual requirements for certain vitamins and minerals. Environment, lifestyle and even genetic factors influence our individual needs, which is why it’s essential to take a personalised approach when it comes to nutrition.

Why your supplements need
to be personalised
Our nutrient requirements evolve as we age and vary for men and women, but these aren’t the only reason we shouldn’t all be taking the same off-the-shelf multivitamins…
  • We all have different dietary requirements

    Whether you’re vegan, vegetarian, suffer from a digestive issue or simply need to avoid certain foods due pregnancy or an intolerance, the amount of vitamins and minerals you get from food will be affected. For example, it’s almost impossible to get enough vitamin B12 on a vegan diet and lactose intolerance can make it more difficult to get enough calcium.

  • We all have different lifestyles

    Although the number of smokers has drastically reduced in recent years (which is good news for vitamin C and E levels), the majority of us still consume alcohol. Even a couple of drinks a week can affect our levels of vitamin B12 and vitamin C. And whilst we should all be exercising regularly, sweating can also take its toll on the body, depleting our stores of key minerals such as magnesium and potassium.

  • We all have different health goals

    Perhaps you’ve been feeling a little stressed recently. Did you know that vitamin C can help the body recover from emotional and physical stress by helping to regulate cortisol levels? Maybe you’re looking to improve your energy levels and sleep better? B vitamins help the body to produce energy and magnesium can contribute to sounder sleep. Whatever your health goal, the right supplements can help get you there.
