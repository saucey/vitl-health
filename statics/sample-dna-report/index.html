What is caffeine-induced anxiety?
For some, caffeine can induce symptoms of anxiety: increased heart rate, feelings of panic, stomach upsets (e.g nausea and diarrhoea), and excessive sweating. The extent of this type of response to consuming caffeine is partly down to your genes.
The biology behind caffeine metabolism
Caffeine is the most commonly used stimulant but it is possible to become dependant on it to feel awake.
The more caffeine you consume, the more caffeine receptors are created in your body. This means that people who regularly consume lots of caffeine can experience withdrawal if they donâ€™t get their regular fix. Symptoms include headaches, tiredness, and feeling weak.
Caffeine is metabolised in the liver and in the case of coffee, it takes approximately 5 minutes to be absorbed into the blood. The final effect is achieved after 30 minutes and its effect is present for between 3-10 hours.
Caffeine has been shown to decrease perceived levels of exertion, making you feel less tired, and it can therefore improve athletic performance.
Caffeine also increases your resting metabolic rate (RMR) and cellular thermogenesis, which can help with weight loss. However, these benefits vary between individuals and are dependant on your rate of metabolism.
